Understanding the Opportunity

Changing domicile from New York to Florida represents one of the most significant financial decisions high-net-worth individuals can make. For Jonathan and Larissa, this transition offers substantial tax savings, asset protection benefits, and lifestyle advantages—but only if executed with precision and thoroughness.

The Domicile Concept

Domicile is your permanent, primary home—the place you intend to return to whenever you're away. Unlike "residence" (where you live), you can have only ONE domicile at a time.

New York law presumes your domicile remains New York until you provide "clear and convincing evidence" that you've abandoned it and established a new one in Florida.

Why NY Fights Back

New York State maintains 300+ auditors whose sole job is challenging domicile changes. They generated over $3 billion from residency audits in 2022-2023 alone.

High-income earners ($1M+) face near-certain audit scrutiny. The state uses cell phone records, E-ZPass data, credit card statements, and even veterinary records to verify your true location.

The Dual Threat

New York uses TWO tests to keep taxing you:

  • Domicile Test: Where you consider "home"
  • Statutory Residency: Maintaining a NY home for 10+ months AND spending 184+ days in NY

You must win BOTH tests to escape NY taxation completely.

Critical Understanding for Jonathan & Larissa

Simply buying a Florida home and getting a Florida driver's license is NOT enough. The recent Matter of Hoff (October 2026) case confirmed this: A couple who obtained Florida licenses, registered to vote, filed declarations of domicile, and spent fewer than 165 days in NY still LOST because they hadn't genuinely reoriented their life around Florida. The tribunal stated: "Formal declarations are less significant than informal acts demonstrating an individual's general habit of life."

The Five Domicile Factors

New York auditors evaluate domicile using five primary factors. No single factor is determinative—success requires winning across ALL five categories. Here's how Jonathan and Larissa should approach each one.

1. Home

What NY Examines: Comparative size, value, and use of all residences you maintain.

Your Strategy:

  • St. Andrews home ($1.7M+) must be your primary residence in every respect
  • Critical: Any retained NY property must be smaller, less valuable, and clearly secondary
  • Ideal: Sell NY property entirely or rent to unrelated third parties for 10+ months
  • Spend major holidays (Thanksgiving, Christmas, New Year's) in Florida
  • Host family gatherings at St. Andrews, not NY

2. Active Business Involvement

What NY Examines: Where you actively manage business operations—often "the most persuasive indicia" of domicile.

Jonathan's Finance Business:

  • Establish a dedicated Florida office (home office + commercial space recommended)
  • Document that core business decisions are made in Florida
  • If NY business continues: Structure as passive investment or shift to Florida entity
  • Use Florida address on all business cards, websites, and marketing materials

Larissa's Home Healthcare Business:

  • Establish Florida-based home healthcare operations
  • Register business entity in Florida
  • If continuing NY operations: Hire NY-based manager to handle day-to-day
  • Document Florida as primary business location

3. Time (Day Counting)

What NY Examines: Physical presence patterns. Even spending under 183 days in NY isn't enough—you must spend the MAJORITY of time in Florida.

Critical Rules:

  • ANY part of a day in NY = full NY day (11pm arrival = 1 day)
  • Target: 200+ days in Florida, under 120 days in NY
  • Keep contemporaneous records (apps like MONAEO track via GPS)
  • Retain all boarding passes, hotel receipts, toll records
  • NY will subpoena E-ZPass, cell tower data, credit card records

4. Items "Near and Dear"

What NY Examines: Location of sentimental and valuable possessions (the "teddy bear test").

What to Move to Florida:

  • Family heirlooms and photo albums
  • Art collections and valuable jewelry
  • Wedding albums, children's baby items
  • Wine collections, hobby equipment
  • Pets (update vet records to Florida)
  • Primary wardrobe and seasonal clothing

Documentation: Keep shipping records, insurance policy updates showing Florida location, moving company receipts.

5. Family Connections

What NY Examines: Where your spouse lives and where children attend school (if applicable).

For Jonathan & Larissa:

  • Both must establish Florida domicile simultaneously
  • Spend time together in Florida (not separate travel patterns)
  • Join Florida-based social organizations as a couple
  • Document Florida as your shared life center

Note: If you have children, enrolling them in Florida schools is one of the strongest domicile indicators. Keeping children in NY schools makes successful domicile change extremely difficult.

The "Convenience of the Employer" Trap

Critical for Jonathan if W-2 employee: If you work remotely for a NY-based employer, NY's "Convenience of the Employer" rule may still tax your wages even as a FL resident. The rule states that remote work performed for your own "convenience" (rather than employer's "necessity") remains NY-source income.

Good news: This rule does NOT apply to self-employed individuals. If you're operating your own finance business (not employed by a NY firm), your income sourcing follows where you physically work.

Recent case (Zelinsky, May 2026): Upheld COTE rule even for remote workers. If you're an employee of NY firm, consider converting to independent contractor or consultant structure.

Fraud vs. Negligence: The Critical Distinction

Negligence: 5% penalty + 50% of interest. Typically applied when taxpayer made "reasonable effort" but failed to meet burden of proof.

Fraud: 200% penalty + interest + no statute of limitations. Applied when taxpayer knowingly filed false returns or concealed information.

What triggers fraud findings: Destroying documents, false statements under oath, maintaining secret NY residence, backdating documents, falsifying day counts.

Your protection: Contemporaneous, honest documentation. Working with SALT attorney demonstrates good faith effort.
